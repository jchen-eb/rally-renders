Run a Mile in Your Undies for a Good Cause

By Daisy Barringer / January 23, 2019
   Chelsea Younkin
   Chelsea Younkin

Picture this: Hundreds or even thousands of people stripping down to their brightest, wackiest, (ahem) flashiest bedroom-best and taking off on a mad dash through their city. Rain or shine, snow or sleet, nothing’s going to stop these motivated runners from baring (almost) all for a “brief” mile-long walk/jog/sprint because they’re doing it for an amazing cause: to raise money and awareness to support those affected by neurofibromatosis (NF).

NF is a genetic disorder of the nervous system that causes tumors to form on nerve tissue anywhere in the nervous system, including the brain and spinal cord. It can have unpredictable effects and varying degrees of severity. The disorder usually appears in childhood, but in unusual cases, it can appear later. That was the case for Drew Leathers, who was diagnosed with a rare variant of NF at the age of 16 and later became paralyzed because of the tumors encroaching on his spine.

Brendan Hanrahan was best friends with Drew’s older brother, Chad, and because of his close relationship with them, he attended lots of events in their hometown of Lilburn, Georgia to raise money for the Children’s Tumor Foundation (CTF), the first grassroots organization dedicated to finding treatments for and ending NF. It was an important and personal cause for Brendan, and when he moved to Washington, D.C. for graduate school in 2010, he decided it was time to start something of his own.

Casey Miller

Finding a way to stand out in the crowd

Brendan wanted to raise money for CTF, but he was a grad student and didn’t have the friend base to support an expensive ball or a golf tournament. “So I had to do something crazy that college kids would get into,” he says. He also knew that if he wanted to get people involved in a town like D.C. where so many charity events take place, he needed to do something to stand out in the crowd and provide people with a unique opportunity.

And what’s more unique than running around in the freezing cold in your underwear? “It would probably be weird to run in your underwear around the U.S. capital, but if you’re doing it for charity with 800 other people, it’s a pretty unique opportunity.” And so, with the help of Chad and another friend, Bobby Gill, Cupid’s Charity and Cupid’s Undie Run were born.

Amit Gabai

Show off your skivvies

Cupid’s Undie Run, which Brendan describes as “first and foremost a charity and next, a party,” is a half-day event that takes place in February in 38 cities around the country.

The event starts around lunchtime and everyone celebrates their fundraising with mingling, music, and drinks. Then, in the middle of the event, everyone strips down to their undies and runs a short course around the city before returning for an awesome dance party. The more people fundraise, the more rewards they earn, and it takes only $250 to get access to an open bar. One hundred percent of Cupid’s net proceeds go to the Children’s Tumor Foundation, and since the first run in 2010, they’ve raised over $16.9 million.

Sound like a great way to have fun and raise money for a good cause? Cupid’s Undie Run is happening all over the United States on February 2, 9, 16, and 23. Register today for $40 and start fundraising!

Amit Gabai

DIY fundraising

Can’t make it in February, but want to get involved? Come up with a wild idea of your own to raise money to #EndNF and Cupid’s will help you make it happen through their DIY fundraising program. “Our organization has the expertise and web infrastructure to help you do whatever crazy idea you have if you want to support our cause,” Brendan says. “The DIY platform is really open ended, and we’re excited for people to come to us with crazy charity ideas and we’ll do our best to make those happen.”

Brunch in your bathrobe

There’s another Cupid’s event in the works for this fall: a Bathrobe Brunch where you and your friends can have a Sunday Funday complete with a champagne-popping catered brunch without ever putting on pants. Brendan says the fundraising is more traditional for that event. “It will be based on ticket sales and we’ll have local fundraising, like silent auctions, on-site.

Alexander Alzona

Making a difference

NF affects one in 3,000 people around the world, and it’s not picky. It doesn’t care about race, ethnicity, or gender: It affects all demographics. It’s actually one of the most common genetic disorders in the United States (half of people inherit it from a parent and the others develop it by chance), and yet, Brendan points out that there isn’t a champion person or event. “Cupid’s is probably the main vehicle for how people learn this disorder exists,” he explains.

Right now, there is no cure, but Brendan hopes that will change and that people will keep Cupid’s in mind as they continue to broaden their charitable interactions with the world.

Oh, and about Drew Leathers? He still has NF; after all, there is no cure. But he’s never given up, and in 2012, he was able to stand on his own two feet to accept the CTF Ambassador Award.
