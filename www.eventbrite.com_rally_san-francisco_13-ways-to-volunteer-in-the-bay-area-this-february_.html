13 Ways to Volunteer in the Bay Area This February

By Daisy Barringer / January 20, 2019
   
   

Looking for fun ways to give back to your community? This month, there are opportunities to work with rescue animals, register new citizens to vote, get out in nature to work on local farms and in local parks and gardens, and more. If you find a volunteer opportunity you want to be a part of, don’t forget to register as spaces tend to fill up fast.

   Hank-deVre for Squaw Alpine

In-Shelter Volunteer Orientation at Family Dog Rescue

February 02, 9:00 AM — 11:30 AM

2253 Shafter Ave

This training will teach you about Family Dog Rescue, how the organization runs, animal behavior, shelter cleanliness, and how to properly walk and socialize our rescue dogs.

FREE / RSVP

Volunteer at the Kaiser Permanente San Francisco Half Marathon

February 03, 5:00 AM — 1:00 PM

Golden Gate Park

Be a part of the volunteer team for the 36th Annual Kaiser Permanente San Francisco Half Marathon. Over 1,200 volunteers are needed to assist runners for the race and pre-raceway tasks.

FREE / RSVP

Interactive Farm Tour and Volunteer Work Party

February 03, 1:00 PM — 5:00 PM

Urban Adamah

Discover urban farming secrets through an interactive farm tour at Urban Adamah's new 2-acre campus in North Berkeley. Then stick around for a volunteer work party!

FREE / RSVP

Volunteer as a Group at Family Dog Rescue

February 06, 1:00 PM — 4:00 PM

Family Dog Rescue

Groups of 4 to 10 volunteer for a three-hour shift that consists of 1.5 hours of cleaning/organizing and 1.5 hours of playing/walking/bathing dogs. Make your reservation ASAP as slots fill up fast!

FREE / RSVP
   American Red Cross Bay Area

Red Cross Volunteer Information Session

February 06, 6:00 PM — 7:00 PM

American Red Cross

Learn about the American Red Cross and volunteer opportunities in Disaster Response, Community Education, Client Casework, Blood Drives, Transportation, Communications, and more.

FREE / RSVP

Community Volunteer Day at San Francisco Botanical Garden

February 09, 9:00 AM — 12:00 PM

San Francisco Botanical Garden

Use your green thumb. Volunteers work with a professional horticulturist to weed, mulch, plant, and maintain the Garden's collections. No experience required. Participation limited to 15 volunteers.

FREE / RSVP

Friends of the Alameda Animal Shelter Volunteer Orientation

February 09, 10:00 AM — 11:30 AM

Friends of the Alameda Animal Shelter

Take the first step in becoming a volunteer at Friends of the Alameda Animal Shelter (FAAS) by attending a 90-minute orientation class.

FREE / RSVP

Volunteer at Foothills Park

February 10, 9:00 AM — 12:00 PM

Foothills Park

Spend the morning outdoors. Enjoy hands-on activities such as weeding, planting, and creating habitat structures that will enrich our environment and leave you with a smile!

FREE / RSVP

Register New Citizens to Vote

February 13, 2:00 PM — 4:00 PM

Paramount Theatre

Join Democracy Action volunteers to help register new citizens to vote following the monthly swearing-in ceremony at the Paramount Theatre in Oakland.

FREE / RSVP

Inner Bair Island Restoration Volunteer Event

February 17, 9:00 AM — 12:00 PM

Inner Bair Island

Volunteers will be guided by SFBBO’s staff and will spend the morning planting over 30 native species that will support Bair Island’s ecosystem.

FREE / RSVP

Volunteer Orientation at Hopalong Animal Rescue

February 23, 11:00 AM — 12:00 PM

Hopalong Animal Rescue

Hopalong offers a variety of ways you can help animals and people, including animal care, helping at mobile adoptions, fostering, and more. The first step is to attend this volunteer orientation.

FREE / RSVP

Lake Merritt Food Pantry Distribution

February 23, 8:30 AM — 12:00 PM

1330 Lakeshore Ave

Volunteers, including those who can serve as language interpreters, are needed to staff Food Pantry distribution from the line set-up to monitoring and distributing food resources to the clean-up.

FREE / RSVP

Volunteer to Work on the Teaching Farm at Slide Ranch

February 23, 9:00 AM — 1:00 PM

Slide Ranch

You'll work under the supervision of trained staff on the teaching farm and 134 acres of wild lands gardening, fixing fences and doing repairs, helping with events, etc. Space is limited to 10 people.

FREE / RSVP
