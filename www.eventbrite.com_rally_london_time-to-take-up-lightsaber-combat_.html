Time to Take Up: Lightsaber Combat

By Thomas Hobbs / January 23, 2019
   
   

What is it?

Ever since Obi Wan Kenobi and Darth Vader artfully whacked one another with luminous sticks in 1979’s Star Wars: A New Hope, the world has been fascinated with lightsabers. Somewhere between fencing and martial arts, this ancient Jedi combat is a defining feature within the Star Wars universe, with Luke Skywalker frequently using his weapon of choice to take down his enemies. Over recent years, lightsaber combat (or LudoSport) classes have popped up across London, giving everyday folk a chance to become a Jedi for the evening.

Why now?

The Star Wars universe has also never been more visible, making this an escapist hobby that will resonate with fans of the franchise across multiple generations. And it beats the gym, doesn’t it?

What equipment do I need?

An ability to tap into “the force” and conduct Jedi mind tricks would be helpful, but isn’t 100% necessary. Most classes provide you with a glowing lightsaber, but that doesn’t mean you can’t buy your own. Dressing up in karate wear is a good idea if you want to look even more like a Jedi Master and feel airy while you’re at it…

Who are the experts and how do I learn?

North London-based SilverSabres is a great place to start, with experienced Tai Chi instructors Faisal Ahmed Mian and Nick Musson providing two-hour classes. Their classes combine classic martial arts techniques with moves from the Star Wars universe, and they encourage dress-up, which means you can go as Darth Maul without judgement. Pineapple Studios offer classes with actor Angela Yeoh. Meanwhile, Andrew Laden, a martial arts expert who was actually the stand-in double for fallen Jedi master (RIP) Qui-Gon Jinn (Liam Neeson) during the filming of 1999’s The Phantom Menace, will be running The Jedi Fight Academy at the Theatro Technis throughout January. Seek him out, you shall.

Who will I meet?

Fellow Star Wars fans are a given, but you’ll also find adults looking for a workout that’s a bit more fun than militant Zumba classes down their local gym. Lightsaber combat is great for shedding weight while having a laugh and making some new mates.

How do I become teacher’s pet?

Patience and an ability to do exactly what your Jedi Master says is a good start. Basically, do the complete opposite to what Luke does during his failed Jedi training in the Empire Strikes Back.
